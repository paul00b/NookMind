How should we group the onboarding slides?

You have 4 great ideas — here are 3 ways to structure them into slides

A

3 Slides — Tight & Punchy

Slide 1: The big picture — Track movies, series & books. Your watchlist, your reading list, all in one place. Free, no ads.

Slide 2: Smart features — IMDB ratings per episode, new episode notifications, cast info, streaming providers, collections & stats.

Slide 3: Discover — Trending now, upcoming releases, find your next obsession.

Pros

  • Fast, no one drops off
  • Each slide has a clear "wow" moment

Cons

  • Slide 2 is dense — lots of features crammed in
B

4 Slides — One Idea Per Slide

Slide 1: The big picture — Track movies, series & books. Free, no ads.

Slide 2: Deep features — IMDB ratings, episode notifications, cast & streaming providers.

Slide 3: Discover — Trending, upcoming, recommendations.

Slide 4: Collections & Stats — Organize, track progress, see your habits.

Pros

  • Each idea gets breathing room
  • Follows your original 4 ideas closely

Cons

  • 4 slides risks some users skipping
  • Collections/stats may not be exciting enough as a standalone closer
C

3 Slides — End on a Bang

Slide 1: The big picture — All your entertainment in one place. Free, no ads.

Slide 2: Never miss a thing — Episode alerts, IMDB ratings, streaming providers, cast info.

Slide 3: Discover & Collect — Trending, upcoming, build collections, track your stats.

Pros

  • Merges Discovery + Collections into a strong final slide
  • 3 slides = fast onboarding
  • Ends with action-oriented features (explore + organize)

Cons

  • Last slide covers two concepts
